Premise

Avatar: The Last Airbender, also known as Avatar: The Legend of Aang in some regions, is an Emmy Award-winning animated television series that aired for three seasons on Nickelodeon. The series was created and produced by Michael Dante DiMartino and Bryan Konietzko.

Avatar is set in a world of Asian martial arts and elemental manipulation. The show drew on elements of Western, South Asian, and East Asian culture, making it a blend of what were previously traditionally separate categories of Japanese anime and Western domestic cartoons.

The series follows the adventures of the main protagonist Aang and his friends, who must save the world by defeating Fire Lord Ozai and ending the destructive war with the Fire Nation. The show first aired on February 21, 2005.

The popularity of the series spawned a sequel series titled "The Legend of Korra," which takes place seventy years after the original series.

Mike and I were really interested in other epic 'Legends & Lore' properties, like Harry Potter and Lord of the Rings, but we knew that we wanted to take a different approach to that type of genre. Our love for Japanese Anime, Hong Kong action & Kung Fu cinema, yoga, and Eastern philosophies led us to the initial inspiration for Avatar.

— Bryan Konietzko about the creation of Avatar: The Last Airbender.
